Fishing Guide

Fish Species

Largemouth Bass

The largemouth bass (Micropterus salmoides) is a top-tier carnivorous freshwater gamefish in the sunfish family, renowned for its ambush predatory behavior and popularity among anglers. Native to eastern North America, they thrive in warm, vegetated, quiet waters. Females are larger than males, with a maximum recorded length of 97 cm.

Where: Warm lakes like Utah Lake

Bait: Worms, crankbaits

Tip: Fish early morning or evening

Rainbow Trout

Rainbow trout (Oncorhynchus mykiss) are a popular freshwater fish known for their colorful appearance, featuring a bluish-green back, silver sides, and a distinctive pink or red stripe, along with black spots. Native to the Pacific Northwest, they thrive in cold, clean, oxygenated water and are a major sport fish, with anadromous (ocean-migrating) forms called steelhead. They are opportunistic feeders, eating insects and other small organisms, and are identifiable by their forked tail and lack of teeth at the base of the tongue.

Where: Strawberry Reservoir

Bait: Powerbait, flies

Tip: Use light line

Catfish

Catfish are a diverse order (Siluriformes) of ray-finned fish recognized by their prominent whisker-like barbels, lack of scales, and smooth or armored skin. Found globally in freshwater and some coastal environments, they are mostly bottom-dwelling scavengers or predators known for an excellent sense of taste and smell.

Where: Utah Lake

Bait: Chicken liver

Tip: Fish at night
