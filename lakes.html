Fishing Guide

Utah Fishing Lakes

Strawberry Reservoir

Strawberry Reservoir ("The Berry") is Utah's premier high-mountain trout fishery, located 23 miles east of Heber on US-40 at roughly 7,600 feet elevation. Known for its trophy-sized Bear Lake cutthroat trout, sterile rainbow trout, and kokanee salmon, it sees over 1.5 million fishing hours annually. It is managed by the Utah Division of Wildlife Resources.

Utah Lake

Utah Lake is a large (148 sq miles), very shallow (avg. 9-10 ft) freshwater lake in Utah Valley. It serves as a vital ecosystem for over 226 bird species, 49 mammal species, and 18 fish species, acting as a crucial breeding stopover along the Pacific Flyway. It is known for its high productivity but faces challenges from invasive carp, harmful algal blooms, and water management.

Bear Lake

Bear Lake is a natural, 28,000-year-old tectonic lake straddling the Utah-Idaho border, renowned as the "Caribbean of the Rockies" for its intense turquoise-blue water. Spanning ~112 square miles (20 miles long, 8 miles wide) with a 208-foot depth, it is famous for unique endemic fish species, sandy beaches, summer recreation, and the "Bear Lake Monster" legend.
